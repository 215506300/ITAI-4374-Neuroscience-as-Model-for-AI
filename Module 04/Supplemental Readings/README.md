# Module 04 — Supplemental Readings

This folder contains all supplemental academic papers assigned for Module 04. These readings deepen understanding of computational neuroscience, spiking models, neuromorphic hardware, and brain‑inspired AI.

---

## Included Papers

### 1. *Computational Neuroscience: A Frontier of the 21st Century*
- Overview of theory, modeling, and the role of computation in neuroscience.
- Emphasizes integration of experimental and theoretical approaches.

### 2. *Computational Neuroscience Approach to Psychiatry*
- Theory‑driven models for psychiatric disorders.
- Phenotypic, neurobiological, and intermediate computational models.

### 3. *We First Need to Understand How the Brain Works if We Want True AI*
- Jeff Hawkins’ Thousand Brains Theory.
- Embodiment, reference frames, and cortical columns.

### 4. *The Hodgkin–Huxley Heritage: From Channels to Circuits*
- Ion channels, action potentials, and computational modeling foundations.

### 5. *The First Computational Theory of Mind and Brain*
- McCulloch & Pitts’ logical calculus.
- Origins of computational neuroscience.

### 6. *Spiking Neural Networks: The Future of Brain‑Inspired Computing*
- SNN models, training methods, energy metrics, and neuromorphic applications.

### 7. *Simpler Can Be Better: Lessons from a Neural Decoding Challenge*
- Neural decoding, continuous learning, robustness, and BMI systems.

---

## Purpose
These readings support:

- Understanding biological grounding of SNNs  
- Connecting neuroscience to AI architectures  
- Exploring neuromorphic hardware capabilities  
- Examining real‑world applications (robotics, BMIs, edge AI)  

---

## How to Use
Students should reference these papers when writing reflections, answering conceptual questions, and connecting module content to broader research.
