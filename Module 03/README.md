# Module 03  
## Brain Anatomy and Computational Neuroscience

### Overview
Module 03 provides foundational knowledge of brain anatomy and introduces computational neuroscience as a framework for understanding how biological neural systems inspire artificial intelligence. The module explains the structural organization of the brain, the physiology of neurons, the mechanisms of neural communication, and the mathematical models used to simulate neural behavior. This module establishes the biological and computational principles that support later modules on learning, networks, and AI system design.

### Learning Objectives
1. Identify major anatomical divisions of the brain and describe their functional roles.  
2. Explain the hierarchical organization of cortical and subcortical systems.  
3. Describe neuron structure, synaptic transmission, and the action potential cycle.  
4. Interpret how biological neurons encode information through spikes.  
5. Understand the Leaky Integrate and Fire model as a mathematical representation of neuronal dynamics.  
6. Connect biological mechanisms to artificial neural networks and modern AI architectures.

### Key Topics
**Brain Structure and Organization**  
The brain is divided into hindbrain, midbrain, and forebrain layers that evolved sequentially. The cerebral cortex contains four lobes with specialized functions. Subcortical structures such as the thalamus, hippocampus, amygdala, and basal ganglia support routing, memory, emotion, and action selection.

**Neuron Anatomy and Physiology**  
Neurons contain dendrites, a soma, an axon, and synaptic terminals. They communicate using all‑or‑nothing action potentials. Synapses adjust their strength through experience, forming the basis of learning.

**Action Potentials and Threshold Dynamics**  
Neurons integrate excitatory and inhibitory inputs. When membrane voltage exceeds threshold, a spike is generated. Refractory periods regulate firing frequency.

**Computational Neuroscience Foundations**  
The Leaky Integrate and Fire model describes membrane voltage dynamics mathematically. Neural coding strategies include rate coding, temporal coding, and population coding. These principles support the design of artificial neural networks.

**Biological Inspiration for AI**  
Hierarchical visual processing parallels convolutional neural networks. Reinforcement learning mechanisms reflect dopaminergic prediction error signals. Neuromorphic computing draws from biological efficiency and event‑driven signaling.
