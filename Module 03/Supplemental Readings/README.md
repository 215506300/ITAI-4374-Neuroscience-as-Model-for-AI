# Module 03 Supplemental Readings  
## Computational Neuroscience Foundations and Applications

### Overview
The supplemental readings for Module 03 provide theoretical, historical, and applied perspectives on computational neuroscience. These materials deepen understanding of how mathematical models, neural circuits, and computational principles explain brain function and support the development of artificial intelligence. The readings also highlight interdisciplinary connections across neuroscience, psychiatry, machine learning, and theoretical biology.

### Reading List and Purpose

#### 1. Computational Neuroscience: A Frontier of the 21st Century  
Purpose:  
- Explains the role of theory and modeling in modern neuroscience.  
- Describes adaptive complex systems, multiscale modeling, and the integration of experimental and computational approaches.  
- Highlights the importance of large‑scale brain circuit modeling and the future of computational neuroscience.

#### 2. Computational Neuroscience Approach to Psychiatry  
Purpose:  
- Introduces theory‑driven models that link neural mechanisms to psychiatric symptoms.  
- Describes phenotypic, neurobiological, and intermediate models.  
- Demonstrates how computational frameworks support diagnosis, prediction, and treatment research.

#### 3. We First Need to Understand How the Brain Works if We Want True AI  
Purpose:  
- Discusses the relationship between biological intelligence and artificial intelligence.  
- Presents the Thousand Brains Theory and the importance of embodiment, sparsity, and continuous learning.  
- Highlights limitations of current AI systems and the need for biologically grounded principles.

#### 4. The Hodgkin‑Huxley Heritage: From Channels to Circuits  
Purpose:  
- Reviews the historical and scientific impact of the Hodgkin‑Huxley model.  
- Explains ion channel structure, action potential mechanisms, and circuit‑level implications.  
- Connects biophysical modeling to modern computational neuroscience.

#### 5. The First Computational Theory of Mind and Brain  
Purpose:  
- Examines the foundational work of McCulloch and Pitts.  
- Explores the origins of neural computation, logical calculus, and early computational models of cognition.  
- Provides historical context for the development of artificial neural networks.

### Key Themes Across Readings
- Mathematical modeling is essential for understanding neural dynamics.  
- Neural systems operate as adaptive, nonlinear, and hierarchical networks.  
- Computational models bridge biological mechanisms and cognitive functions.  
- Historical theories continue to influence modern AI and neuroscience.  
- Interdisciplinary collaboration accelerates progress in brain research.

### How These Readings Support the Module
- Reinforce anatomical and physiological concepts introduced in the main module.  
- Provide deeper insight into neural modeling, from ion channels to large‑scale circuits.  
- Demonstrate how computational neuroscience informs AI architectures and learning algorithms.  
- Highlight real‑world applications in psychiatry, robotics, and machine intelligence.  
- Connect historical foundations to contemporary research directions.

### Integration Notes
These readings should be used to:  
- Strengthen conceptual understanding of neural computation.  
- Support analysis of biological versus artificial neural systems.  
- Prepare for advanced modules on learning, networks, and AI system design.  
- Build interdisciplinary literacy across neuroscience, mathematics, and computer science.
