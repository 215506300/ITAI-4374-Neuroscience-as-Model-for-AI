# Contents

## 1. The Algonauts Project 2021 Challenge  
**File:** `The Algonauts Project 2021 Challenge How the Human Brain Makes Sense of a World in Motion.pdf`

### What This Paper Covers
This paper introduces a large‑scale neuroscience challenge: predicting human brain activity (fMRI) while participants watch short videos.  
It provides:

- Whole‑brain fMRI dataset  
- Explanation of voxel‑wise encoding models  
- How computational models (CNNs, video models) map onto brain responses  
- Benchmarking methods for evaluating brain‑like intelligence  

### Why It Matters for Module 02
Module 02 focuses on **world models** and **how the brain interprets dynamic sensory input**.  
This paper shows:

- How the brain processes **real‑world motion**  
- How AI models attempt to replicate this  
- Why prediction is central to perception  
- How neuroscience evaluates whether an AI model is “brain‑like”

### Key Takeaway
The Algonauts Challenge demonstrates that **understanding the brain requires predictive models**, not just descriptive theories — directly reinforcing Module 02’s emphasis on predictive coding and world modeling.

## 2. Neuronal Shape & Neuronal Information Processing  
**File:** `uen.pressbooks.pub-Neuronal Shape and Neuronal Information Processing.pdf`

### What This Reading Covers
This chapter explains how **neuron morphology determines computation**, including:

- Unipolar, bipolar, and multipolar neurons  
- Dendritic integration  
- Action potential initiation  
- Synaptic transmission  
- Cytoarchitectonics (layered + columnar organization)  
- Purkinje cells, pyramidal neurons, and retinal circuits  

### Why It Matters for Module 02
Module 02 introduces:

- Cortical columns  
- Reference frames  
- Distributed processing  
- Predictive world models  

This reading provides the **biological foundation** for those ideas:

- Why cortical columns exist  
- How neuron shape constrains information flow  
- Why dendrites matter for computation  
- How local vs. long‑range connections shape perception  

### Key Takeaway
**Function follows form.**  
The structure of neurons and cortical columns directly shapes how the brain builds and updates its internal model of the world.
