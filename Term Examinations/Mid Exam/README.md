## 🧠 Audited System

**Microsoft Copilot** — a conversational AI assistant powered by large language model architecture, accessed through a browser-based interface. Copilot was chosen for its accessibility, its positioning as a general-purpose cognitive assistant, and its suitability for repeated, controlled experimentation.

---

## 📐 Report Structure

The midterm report follows the required professional prose format and is organized into four sections:

**1. System Profile**
Describes Microsoft Copilot's architecture, purpose, access method, and rationale for selection. Establishes the framing: Copilot does not possess biological neurons or internal states, but its behavior frequently resembles human cognitive functions at a surface level. The audit evaluates where those surface resemblances hold and where they break down.

**2. Experiment Results**
Four hands-on experiments, each tied to a distinct course module:

| Module | Concept Tested | Key Finding |
|---|---|---|
| **M02 — Brain Architecture** | Parallel vs. serial processing | Copilot handles multi-task prompts but errors (verb misclassification, noun duplication) increase under load — consistent with serial, not parallel, processing |
| **M04 — Memory & Learning** | Working memory and cross-session retention | Strong within-session context retention; cross-session recall uses an engineered durable memory feature, not biological consolidation |
| **M06 — Emotion & Motivation** | Tone modulation, sarcasm detection, emotional framing | Explicit tone-switching was strong; sarcasm was not detected; motivational framing produced more elaborate outputs |
| **M07 — Attention & Consciousness** | Selective attention and noise filtering | Clean noise filtering under explicit instruction; implicit coherence-based filtering also observed; no biological salience mechanism |

Each experiment includes the neuroscience hypothesis tested, step-by-step procedure, observed results, and interpretation grounded in course content. Figures A1–A14 in the appendix provide screenshots of all Copilot responses.

**3. Next Steps**
Plans for the final: adding Module 03 (Perception & Vision) with visual edge-case experiments, deepening existing module experiments, developing the Brain vs. AI Scorecard, writing the Brain-Inspired Redesign section, and recording the video demonstration.

**4. References**
Five peer-reviewed sources cited throughout the report, including Gazzaniga et al. (2019), Squire & Wixted (2011), and Desimone & Duncan (1995).
