# Module 5 — Supplemental Readings

This folder contains six supplemental readings that explore how the brain encodes, processes, and responds to sensory information — spanning foundational neuroscience, clinical applications, consciousness theory, and connections to artificial intelligence.

---

## Readings

### 1. Hacking the Predictive Mind
**Source:** *Brain* (Oxford University Press), 2024 — Book review by Stephen M. Fleming (UCL)
**Reviews:** *The Experience Machine* by Andy Clark (2023)

A review of Andy Clark's book on predictive processing, the framework proposing that the brain is not a passive receiver of sensory input but actively generates predictions about the world and updates them when errors arise. Covers hierarchical prediction architectures, active inference (Karl Friston), precision-weighting, and clinical implications for conditions like functional neurological disorders, autism, schizophrenia, and chronic pain. Also touches on consciousness, placebo effects, and the limits of the predictive processing paradigm.

**Key concepts:** Predictive processing, prediction error, precision-weighting, active inference, top-down vs. bottom-up processing

---

### 2. Sensory Integration
**Source:** Physio-Pedia

A clinical and developmental overview of sensory integration (SI) theory, originally developed by A. Jean Ayres. Covers the major sensory systems (tactile, proprioceptive, vestibular, auditory, visual, olfactory, gustatory, interoceptive), how they are organized and integrated by the brain, and what happens when integration breaks down. Includes a section on sensory processing challenges in children with cerebral palsy and a guide to Sensory Integration Therapy (SIT).

**Key concepts:** Sensory integration, exteroception, proprioception, interoception, vestibular system, hyper/hypo-responsiveness, sensory integration therapy, Pyramid of Learning

---

### 3. Recurrent Processing Theory and the Function of Consciousness
**Source:** Self Aware Patterns (blog), January 2020

An accessible examination of Victor Lamme's Recurrent Processing Theory (RPT) of consciousness. RPT proposes that recurrent (feedback) processing in sensory brain regions — not just widespread cortical broadcasting — is sufficient for conscious experience. The piece compares RPT to Global Workspace Theory (GWT) and Integrated Information Theory (IIT), evaluates Lamme's arguments (including evidence from split-brain patients), and raises philosophical questions about what recurrent processing alone can tell us about subjective experience.

**Key concepts:** Recurrent processing, feedforward vs. feedback processing, consciousness, global workspace theory, introspection, split-brain patients

---

### 4. Sensory Perception Relies on Fitness-Maximizing Codes
**Source:** *Nature Human Behaviour*, Volume 7, July 2023 — Schaffner, Bao, Tobler, Hare & Polania

An empirical research article arguing that sensory systems do not simply maximize perceptual accuracy — they encode information in ways that maximize the organism's fitness (survival and reward). Using behavioral experiments with human participants, blowfly retinal neuron data, and artificial neural networks (ANNs), the authors demonstrate that early sensory structures flexibly adapt their encoding strategies depending on the reward context. fMRI data confirm that these fitness-maximizing codes emerge in early visual areas (V1–V3).

**Key concepts:** Efficient coding, fitness maximization, power-law efficient codes, Fisher information, reward maximization vs. accuracy maximization, early sensory adaptation, retinotopic specificity

---

### 5. Bridging Neuroscience and AI: Insights From the Retina and Beyond
**Source:** Snowflake Engineering Blog

A practitioner-oriented overview of the NeuroAI field, describing how insights from the retina and primary visual cortex (V1) inform AI architecture design. Covers how biological principles have historically shaped AI (perceptrons, CNNs, reinforcement learning), how modern architectures like transformers map onto brain mechanisms, and how retina and V1 modeling research — including work on freely moving mice — reveals the importance of behavioral context in visual processing. Connects these principles to practical AI challenges like text-to-SQL.

**Key concepts:** NeuroAI, retinal modeling, V1 modeling, convolutional neural networks, recurrent neural networks, information compression, multimodal processing

---

### 6. The Highly Sensitive Brain: An fMRI Study of Sensory Processing Sensitivity and Response to Others' Emotions
**Source:** *Brain and Behavior*, 2014 — Acevedo, Aron, Aron, Sangster, Collins & Brown

An fMRI study examining the neural basis of Sensory Processing Sensitivity (SPS) — the trait found in roughly 20% of humans (and over 100 other species) associated with deeper processing of environmental and social stimuli. Participants viewed photos of romantic partners and strangers displaying happy, sad, or neutral expressions. Higher SPS scores were consistently associated with greater activation in brain regions linked to awareness, empathy, sensory integration, and action planning (cingulate, insula, IFG, PMA, MTG). Results replicated across two time points one year apart.

**Key concepts:** Sensory processing sensitivity (SPS), Highly Sensitive Person (HSP) scale, fMRI, empathy, mirror neuron system, insula, anterior cingulate, premotor area, social emotion processing
